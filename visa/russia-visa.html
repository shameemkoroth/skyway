Russia Visa for Indian Travelers: Complete Guide

Planning to explore Moscow, St. Petersburg, or the scenic landscapes of Russia? Indian travelers require a visa to enter Russia.

Skyway Tours and Travels provides expert assistance to make your Russia visa application simple, fast, and hassle-free.

Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Russia?

Yes, Indian passport holders require a visa to enter Russia. Applications can be submitted through authorized agencies or the Russian Embassy.

Types of Russia Visas for Indian Travelers

1. Tourist Visa

  • Best for: Tourism and sightseeing
  • Validity: Usually up to 30 days (single or double entry)
  • Processing Time: 5–10 working days
  • Requirement: Invitation letter from a Russian travel agency or hotel

2. Business Visa

  • Best for: Business meetings, conferences, or trade visits
  • Validity: Up to 90 days (single/multiple entry)
  • Requirement: Invitation letter from a Russian company

3. Transit Visa

  • Best for: Passing through Russia to another country
  • Validity: Usually 2–10 days

Russia Visa Processing Time

  • Tourist Visa: 5–10 working days
  • Business Visa: 7–10 working days
  • Transit Visa: 3–5 working days

Skyway Tip: Apply at least 10–14 days before your travel date to avoid delays.

Important Notes

  • Passport must be valid for at least 6 months from the date of entry.
  • Carry a printed copy of your visa and supporting documents.
  • Ensure your visa type matches the purpose of your travel.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Can I get a Russia visa on arrival?

No, Indian citizens must apply before travel via embassy or authorized agency.

2. How long is a Russia tourist visa valid?

Typically up to 30 days, single or double entry.

3. What documents are required for a Russia tourist visa?

Passport valid for at least 6 months, invitation letter, travel itinerary, and passport-size photograph.

4. How long does it take to process a Russia visa?

Tourist visas take 5–10 working days; business visas take 7–10 days.

5. Can I extend my Russia visa?

Extensions are generally only possible under special circumstances through Russian immigration authorities.
